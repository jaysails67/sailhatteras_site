Year 1 Operations & Financial Summary
Business Concept
PamliEcoConnect operates high‑speed, 20‑passenger research and development ferries linking Vandemere, Ocracoke (Silver Lake), and Buxton. The service is designed to prove out an electrified, environmentally conscious ferry model while providing reliable regional transportation.

Key Operating Assumptions
Ticket price: $35 per passenger
Capacity: 20 passengers per boat
Boats in service:
Summer: 4 boats
Shoulder season: 3 boats
Off‑season (Pamlico): 2 boats
Off‑season alternate southern location: 4 boats
Trips per day (per boat):
Summer: 8
Shoulder season: 6
Off‑season: 4
Southern location: 8
Season lengths: 120 days each for summer, shoulder, off‑season, and southern season
Occupancy & weather factors:
Summer occupancy: 90%
Shoulder & off‑season occupancy: 75%
Safe‑weather factors applied by season (higher in summer, lower in winter)
Year 1 Demand & Revenue
Seats sold (blended year‑round, optimized with winter relocation):
Base NC model: ~110k seats sold
Shared winter location model: ~177k–196k seats sold (depending on weather/occupancy)
Ticket sales (Year 1):
Total ticket revenue: $6,284,964
No material revenue is assumed yet from advertising or concessions in Year 1; upside from those channels is incremental to the base case.

Year 1 Profitability
The pro forma cash flow shows strong cash generation in the first full operating year:

Total ticket revenue: $6,284,964
Total operating disbursements: $2,808,672
Year‑end operating profit (EBITDA‑like): $3,476,292
This figure is effectively EBITDA, as interest, taxes, and depreciation/amortization are not broken out separately in the Year 1 model.

Strategic Implications
The model demonstrates that a small fleet of 20‑passenger R&D ferries can generate multi‑million‑dollar annual revenue with robust operating margins.
Winter relocation to a southern market smooths seasonality, improves asset utilization, and significantly increases annual tickets sold and revenue.
Strong cash flow in Year 1 provides capacity to:
Reinvest in additional vessels,
Upgrade charging and dock infrastructure, and
Expand marketing and partnership programs.
