What You Are Looking At
This is the PamliEcoConnect investor business plan, current as of May 2026. It describes a company being built from the ground up on Hatteras Island, North Carolina, to develop and operate the first clean, fast, electric passenger ferry service on Pamlico Sound — and to manufacture the Jones Act-compliant foiling vessel platform that makes it possible.
The plan is written for sophisticated investors who want to understand not just the opportunity, but the structure, the risks, the financials, and the governance behind it. It does not oversell. It does not hide the hard parts. It is designed to give you what you need to make an informed decision.

Where We Are Right Now
The company is currently raising its Seed Round of $1,000,000 in Series Seed Preferred Stock.
The hull molds are built and on hand at Phillips Boatworks on Hatteras Island. The founding team is in place. The USCG engagement strategy is mapped. The Chesapeake Bay consortium — a five-county, federally funded passenger ferry initiative — has validated the market and is aligned with our vessel platform through our CFO's 30-year network in that region. The grant pipeline is identified, prioritized, and ready to pursue the moment the company is formally capitalized.
What the seed round buys is simple: it builds the first four vessels, begins the USCG certification process, improves the Buxton terminal, and funds the transition of our founding team to full-time PamliEcoConnect operations.

What Comes Next
The seed round closes and boats begin construction. Vessels enter service as 6-passenger uninspected vessels immediately. USCG 20-passenger certification is targeted within 12 months. The first full operating season follows. Vessel sales to third-party operators begin in Year 2. The Bridge round of $3 to $5 million opens in Year 2 once route economics are demonstrated. The Series B of $15 to $20 million targets Year 4 when the 50-passenger flagship is certified and the Chesapeake Bay vessel supply opportunity is ready to execute.

A Note from the Founder
I have been on the water on Pamlico Sound for most of my life. I built my first boat on Hatteras Island. I have coached competitive sailors, run a sail loft, operated a boatyard, and chaired a maritime nonprofit. I did not arrive at this idea from a spreadsheet. I arrived at it from decades of watching a region that should have clean, fast water transportation make do with long drives and car ferries.
The technology exists. The market exists. The regulatory pathway exists. The Chesapeake Bay consortium has already proven that a federally funded program is ready to buy exactly what we are building. What exists now, with this seed round, is the opportunity to be the first ones to build it — on Hatteras Island, by people who belong here, for communities that have been waiting for it.
I am asking for $1,000,000 to start. I am contributing the molds, the expertise, and the relationships. I am putting my name and my reputation behind this. I would be honored to have you alongside us.
