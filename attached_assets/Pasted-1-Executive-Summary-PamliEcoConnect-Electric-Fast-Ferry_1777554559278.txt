1. Executive Summary
PamliEcoConnect – Electric Fast Ferry for Pamlico Sound
PamliEcoConnect is an electric fast‑ferry initiative designed specifically for Pamlico Sound. The project envisions a family of 12–50 passenger eco‑efficient ferries, with a flagship class in the 40–50 passenger range, providing quiet, low‑wake, high‑efficiency passenger service between key Inner Banks (IBX) and Outer Banks (OBX) communities.
Unlike a generic Eastern North Carolina high‑speed catamaran network, PamliEcoConnect is:
Geographically focused on Pamlico Sound as a flagship corridor.
Technologically advanced, using electric or hybrid‑electric propulsion with hydrofoil/foil‑assist where appropriate to reduce drag and wake.
Structurally two‑layered:
A commercial operating arm running scheduled and charter services.
A mission‑aligned arm (non‑profit or foundation partner) focused on decarbonization, research, education, and community access.
The concept builds on years of local boatbuilding and on‑the‑water operations experience through Phillips Boatworks (Hatteras Island) and partner organizations active on Pamlico Sound.
Pamlico Sound today is served primarily by:
Long, indirect highway routes, and
Vehicle ferries optimized for cars and trucks, not walk‑on passenger mobility.
There is currently no electric or hydrofoil‑based passenger service on the Sound. PamliEcoConnect aims to be the first clean, fast passenger connection across Pamlico Sound, integrating:
Commuter and resident travel,
Tourism and eco‑tourism,
Education, training, and research programs.
Initial operations will focus on one or two core routes where:
Water transit offers a real travel‑time advantage versus road/ferry combinations,
There is existing tourism and local demand, and
Terminals can realistically support dock access and, over time, shore‑power charging.
The vessel platform is designed around:
Electric or hybrid‑electric propulsion,
Hydrofoil or foil‑assisted hull forms to reduce drag and wake where appropriate,
USCG‑compliant passenger vessel standards, and
Shallow‑draft, low‑wake operation suitable for Pamlico Sound.
Financially, the project anticipates:
Higher upfront CapEx than a conventional diesel workboat, due to electric and foil technology;
Lower long‑run operating costs per passenger‑mile;
A capital stack that blends private investment, public infrastructure/program support, and mission‑aligned funding (grants, climate and resilience funds).
PamliEcoConnect is designed as a repeatable platform. Once the first vessel and route are proven on Pamlico Sound, the same technology and operating model can extend to:
Additional Pamlico routes, and
Comparable corridors such as Chesapeake Bay, where a five‑county tourism consortium has already validated demand and economic benefit for 50‑passenger hybrid‑electric ferries.
