Executive Summary: PamliEcoConnect “Fly Above the Water. Zero Emissions. Pure Speed.”
The Opportunity & Vision PamliEcoConnect is a pioneering clean-technology transportation initiative designed to bring high-speed, zero-emission electric foiling passenger vessels to North Carolina’s Pamlico Sound
. By replacing long, indirect highway routes and slow, car-optimized ferries with quiet, low-wake electric fast-ferries, this project creates a critical "water highway" linking Inner Banks (IBX) and Outer Banks (OBX) communities
.
For a North Carolina or Research Triangle Park investor, PamliEcoConnect represents an opportunity to fund a highly visible, locally grounded "flagship project" that blends cutting-edge maritime tech, strong regional economic impact, and highly attractive unit economics
.
The Market Gap & Technological Advantage
Uncontested Market Niche: There is currently no electric or hydrofoil-based passenger service on Pamlico Sound
. Existing water mobility relies on vehicle ferries and a patchwork of small tour boats
.
Advanced Platform: Vessels will use electric or hybrid-electric propulsion combined with hydrofoil/foil-assist technology to drastically reduce drag, energy use, and shoreline wake
.
Dual-Layered Business Model: The initiative is uniquely structured with both a commercial operating arm (for-profit) and a mission-aligned partner layer (non-profit/foundation) to capture both commercial revenues and philanthropic/grant capital for decarbonization and research
.
Highly Attractive Financial & Operational Model The initial rollout utilizes a 12–24 passenger R&D class of vessels to prove the concept before scaling to a 50-passenger flagship class
. The Year 1 base model provides extremely compelling economics:
Strong Year 1 Profitability: A Year 1 scenario modeling an inaugural route (Vandemere – Ocracoke – Buxton) with a 20-passenger R&D fleet projects approximately 6.28millioninticketrevenue∗∗and∗∗3.48 million in operating profit (EBITDA)
.
Optimized Asset Utilization: A planned winter relocation of the vessels to a southern market smooths out seasonal demand, improving asset utilization and significantly boosting annual revenue
.
Fast Breakeven: Commercial breakeven is targeted within 3 to 5 years after launch
.
Lower Long-Run Costs: Electrical energy costs combined with foiling efficiencies are expected to be stable and lower per passenger-mile than conventional diesel workboats
.
Scalability & Strategic Expansion
A Repeatable Platform: PamliEcoConnect is designed to scale. Once proven in North Carolina, the exact same technology and operating model can be exported to comparable U.S. coastal corridors
.
Ready-to-Go Expansion Market: Regional partners in the Chesapeake Bay have already validated demand (forecasting 50,000 annual passengers and $11M in economic impact) for similar 50-passenger hybrid-electric ferries, positioning PamliEcoConnect perfectly for sister deployments
.
Non-Dilutive Funding & Public-Private Partnerships (P3)
Grant Readiness: The company is aggressively positioned to capture massive non-dilutive federal and state capital, including NSF SBIR/STTR grants, DOE/ARPA-E maritime electrification funds, and MARAD Small Shipyard grants
. Small business waivers also allow for a 50% cost-share waiver on U.S. Navy/NSRP projects
.
Strategic P3 Alignment: The plan anticipates strong collaboration with the NCDOT Ferry Division, Visit NC, and local governments to share infrastructure planning, terminal costs, and marketing efforts
.
